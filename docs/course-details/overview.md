# Course overview

## About this course

This is a practical, beginner-friendly introduction to **Google Earth Engine
(GEE)** for climate data, designed for the Pacific. It was developed for the
**SPREP / UNEP CIS-Pac5 Regional Capacity Building on GIS & Data Management**
programme (2026).

The course is deliberately hands-on. Every concept is paired with a script you
can copy and paste, and every exercise ends with a map or chart for your own
country.

## Who it's for

- GIS officers, data officers and technical staff in Pacific Island countries
  and territories.
- Anyone who wants to use free, global climate data without buying software or
  downloading huge files.
- No programming experience is assumed. You will read and edit a little
  JavaScript, but the scripts are written to be changed one line at a time.

## What you'll learn

1. Create a free Earth Engine account linked to a Google Cloud project.
2. Find your way around the Earth Engine Code Editor.
3. Load and clip global climate datasets to your country.
4. Map **rainfall and drought** (GPM IMERG).
5. Map **temperature and heat** and the warming trend (ERA5, MODIS).
6. Map **sea-surface temperature** and reef heat stress (NOAA OISST).
7. Flag **low-lying coast** exposed to sea-level rise (NASADEM, JRC Surface Water).
8. Export your results for use in QGIS / ArcGIS.

## How it's structured

  - **Google Earth Engine** — Earth Engine concepts and the datasets we use.
- **Exercises** — five hands-on activities that build on each other.
- **Scripts & code** — every script, ready to copy, in JavaScript and Python.
- **Resources** — glossary, dataset reference, downloads and a validation report.

## Time required

About 2.5–3 hours for all five exercises, or one workshop day with discussion
and your own data.
